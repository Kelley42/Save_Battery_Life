# Tips to extend battery life on Android phone
### Using Samsung Galaxy S10e model
---

Knowing how to squeeze every last drop out of your phone may save you when your battery is at 5% and you need to make an important phone call. But it’s also good to keep these settings on all the time to prevent constantly having to find somewhere to charge your phone throughout the day.

## Settings
To begin, first go to Settings.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/settings_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Settings"/>

## Connections
Go to Connections. Here, you can change how your phone uses data and how it connects to other devices.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/connections_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Connections"/>

- Go to More connection settings. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/more_connection_settings_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="More connection settings"/>

- Make sure Nearby device scanning is toggled off. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/nearby_device_scanning_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Toggle off earby device scanning"/>

- Go to Printing. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/printing_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Printing"/>

Make sure Default Print Service is toggled off.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/default_print_service_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Toggle off Default Print Service"/>

## Display
Go back to Settings and scroll down to Display. Here, you can dim the display of your phone. //////////

- Selecting Dark mode will help save battery.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/dark_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Dark mode"/>

- If you only want Dark mode at night, go to Dark mode settings instead.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/dark_mode_settings_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Dark mode settings"/>

 Toggle on Turn on as scheduled, which will allow you to set it to Sunset to sunrise or to Custom Schedule.

 <img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/turn_onas_scheduled_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Turn on as scheduled"/>

- Slide the Brightness slider to lower the brightness and save battery. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/brightness_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Adjust brightness"/>

You can also do this from the shortcut, found by sliding down twice from the top of the display.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/brightness_shortcut_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Adjust brightness shortcut"/>

- Scroll down to go to Screen timeout. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/screen_timeout_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Screen timeout"/>

Select a lower time, such as 30 seconds, so the display will turn off sooner.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/display_timer_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Set screen timeout"/>

- Back in Display, scroll down to the bottom and go to Screen saver. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/screensaver_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Screensaver"/>

Make sure None is selected.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/none_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Select none"/>

## Lock screen
Go back to Settings and scroll down to Lock screen. Here, you can control what your screen shows when it’s locked.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/lock_screen_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Lock screen"/>

- Toggle Always On Display to off. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/always_on_display_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Always On Display"/>

Alternatively, press the Always On Display heading to customize the display. If you only want to display information sometimes, toggle it On and choose Tap to show so the display will only show when you want it to.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/tap_to_show_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Choose Tap to show"/>

## Location
Go back to Settings and scroll down to Location. Here, you can control which apps know your location. ////////
- Go to Location services. ////////

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/location_services_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Location services"/>

Earthquake alerts, Emergency Location Service, and Google Location Accuracy should read On, while Google Location History and Google Location Sharing should read Tap to manage.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/google_location_history_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Toggling off Google Location History and Sharing"/>

- Toggle off both Wi-Fi scanning and Bluetooth scanning. The more your phone is constantly scanning for nearby devices, the more battery it will use.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/wifi_bluetooth_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Toggle off wi-fi and bluetooth"/>

## Advanced features
Go back to Settings and scroll down to Advanced features.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/advanced_features_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Advanced features"/>

- Go to Motions and gestures. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/motions_gestures_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Motions and gestures"/>

Toggle off Lift to wake and Keep screen on while viewing.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/lift_to_wake_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Toggle off Lift to wake and Keep screen on while viewing"/>

## Battery and device care
Go back to Settings and scroll down to Battery and device care. //////
- Go to Battery, which shows a detailed graph of your usage from your last charge.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/great_battery_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Select battery"/>

- In Background usage limits, make sure Put unused apps to sleep is toggled on.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/put_to_sleep_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Toggle on Put unused apps to sleep"/>

- Back in Battery, scroll down to More battery settings and toggle Adaptive battery on. To preserve the overall health of your battery, toggle off both Fast charging and Fast wireless charging and toggle on Protect battery.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/adaptive_battery_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Adaptive battery, charging, and protect battery"/>

## Apps
Go back to Settings and scroll down to Apps. ///////
- Press the 3 dots in the top right corner to go to Special access. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/apps_dots_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Apps three dots"/>

Scroll down to Usage data access.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/usage_data_access_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Usage data access"/>

Toggle off Digital Wellbeing. While a helpful app, it does track a lot of movement and location information and can use up more battery.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/digital_wellbeing_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Toggle off Digital Wellbeing"/>

## Software update
Finally, go back to Settings and scroll down to Software update to make sure you have the latest software.

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/software_update_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Software update"/>

- Check that it says You’re all set! at the bottom. If it doesn’t, go to Check for software updates to install the latest software. 

<img src="images/Save_Battery_Life_images/youre_all_set_samsung.jpg" width=180 alt="Check for software updates"/>
